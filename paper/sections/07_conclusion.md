# 7. Conclusion

We introduced Baihu v2.0, a large-scale, multi-embodiment robot manipulation dataset for embodied foundation model pretraining and evaluation. Baihu v2.0 contains 9521.75 hours of robot data, 2989 tasks, 513575 episodes, and over 1.03 billion frames. By organizing multi-source robot operation data into a standardized LeRobot-compatible format, Baihu provides a scalable data foundation for training vision-language-action models and generalist robot policies.

The current analysis shows that Baihu v2.0 is not only large in scale, but also diverse across robot embodiment tags. Its long-tailed embodiment distribution makes it suitable for studying both high-resource policy learning and low-resource cross-embodiment generalization. We believe Baihu can serve as an important infrastructure for future embodied intelligence research, especially for large-scale robot policy pretraining, task generalization, and data-driven robot foundation model development.
